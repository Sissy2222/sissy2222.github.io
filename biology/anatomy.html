Adductor longus

Meaning


adduct = draw toward
longus = long

Function


adducts & flexes the thigh
at the hip

Origin


upper pubis, below
the pubic tubercle

Insertion


in the linear third of the
linea aspera of the femur       

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

Adductor magnus

Meaning


  adduct = to draw toward
magnus = great


Function


  adducts hip towards
bodies midline

Origin


  outer surface of lower
pubic ramus & ischial ramus

Insertion


  at the adductor tubercle
   on the medial
femoral condyle


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Biceps femoris


Meaning





bicept = double-headed
or two peaks
femoris = femur =
long bone of upper leg


Function


one of three hamstring muscles
extends thigh and
bends & rotates the knee

Origin





back of upper leg (hamstring)
facet of the ischial tuberosity

Insertion


head of the fibula


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Extensor digitorum longus


Meaning





ex = out from
tendere = to stretch
digitorum = digitus =
pointing thing
longus = long


Function


allows foot and toes to be
bend upwards (stretching)

Origin





at shaft of fibula
shin

Insertion


into three middle toes


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Fibularus longus


Meaning





fibularus = calf of the leg
longus = long


Function


allows for flexing the ankles

Origin





outermost muscle of calves

at lateral shaft of
the fibula and the
intermuscular septa

Insertion


medial cuneiform bone
bone leading to big toe


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Flexor digitorum longus


Meaning





flexor = to bend
digitorum = digitus =
pointing thing
longus = long


Function


allows for flexing and inverting
foot allows for curling of
2nd, 3rd and 4th toe

Origin





at posterior surface
of the tibia

Insertion


toes (all but
the big toe)

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Gastrocnemius


     Meaning





      gastrcnemius =
      stomach of the leg


Function


    flexing the foot at the ankle
   and flexing the leg at the knee

Origin





   medial and lateral
   condyle of femur

Insertion


tendo calcaneus
(achilles tendon)


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Gracilis


     Meaning





       grasilis = slender, slim


Function


    adducts hip and
    helps to flex knees

Origin





    lower ischial pubic ramus
    (pubic bone)

Insertion


    tuberosity (large rounded
    part of bone) of tibia
along with the Sartorius &
Semitendinosus


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Pectineus


    Meaning





     pectineus = comb


Function


   flexes hip, and
adducts & rotates thigh

Origin





   pectineal line of pubis

Insertion


   pectineal line of the femur


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Piriformis


    Meaning





   piriformis = pear shaped


Function


   helps rotate hip
   abducts thigh &
   stabilizes head of femur

Origin





   inner sacrum

Insertion


   trochanter (two bony
   protuberances for muscle
   attachment) of femur


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Plantaris


    Meaning





      planta = sole of the foot


Function


flexes knees and
helps flex ankles

Origin





    lower head ridge of femur,
just above the gastrocnemius origin

Insertion


     Achilles tendon


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Rectus femoris


Meaning





rectus = straight
femoris = femur = long bone of upper leg


Function


flexes hip
helps to extend and raise the knee

Origin





hip (iliac spine)

Insertion


patella of the knee


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Sartorius


    Meaning





     sartor = tailor
     sartorius = tailors muscle


Function


    is a synergist muscle
weak actions
flex, abduct & rotate hip
felexes knee

Origin





    iliac spine of the
pelvic bone

Insertion


    tuberosity (large rounded
part of bone) of tibia
along with the Gracilis &
Semitendinosus


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Semimembranosus


   Meaning





   semi = half
   membrana = skin


Function


  one of three hamstring muscles
flexes and rotates leg

Origin





   upper back quadrant
of the ischial tuberosity

Insertion


   back of tibial condyle
(rounded protuberance
at end of bone) surface


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Semitendinosus


   Meaning





   semi = half
   tendere = to stretch


Function


   one of three hamstring muscles
flexes knee and extends hip

Origin





   inner base of pelvis

Insertion


   tuberosity (large rounded
part of bone) of tibia
along with the Sartorius &
Gracilis


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Soleus


    Meaning





      soleus = flat sandal


Function


    plantarflexion (flexing downward)
of foot and toes

Origin





   back of fibular head

Insertion


   firbous tissue of
gastrocnemius
forms the Achilles tendon


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Tensor fasciae latae


Meaning





tensor = stretcher
fasciae = of the band
the stretcher of the side band


Function


abducts & rotates femur
braces the knee

Origin





back of fibular head

Insertion


iliotibial tract (IT band)
forms the Achilles tendon


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Tibialis anterior


   Meaning





   tibia = shinbone
   anterior = front, fore


Function


dorsiflexion (flexing upward)
and inverts foot

Origin





upper half of front shaft
of the tibia

Insertion


wedge and base of the
1st metatarsal


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Tibialis posterior


   Meaning





   tibia = shinbone
   posterior = behind


Function


plantarflexion (flexing downward)
and inversion of foot
supports arch of foot

Origin





inner borders
of the tibia and fibula

Insertion


base of metatarsel
two - four


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Vastus intermedius


   Meaning





   vastus = enormous
   intermedius = middle


Function


facilitates knee extension

Origin





upper femur
(the thighbone)

Insertion


quadriceps tendon
(connecting femoris to patella)


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Vastus lateralis


   Meaning





   vastus = enormous
   lateralis = belonging to the side


  Function


    extend lower leg

  Origin





    upper femur
    (the thighbone)

  Insertion


     quadriceps tendon
    (connecting femoris to patella)


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Vastus medialus


   Meaning





   vastus = enormous
   medialus = middle


  Function


    knee extension
    stabilizes patella

  Origin





    upper femoral shaft

  Insertion


     quadriceps tendon
    (connecting femoris to patella)
