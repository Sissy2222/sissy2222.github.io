"The whole purpose of education

 is to turn mirrors into windows."



               

Sydney J Harris

                  
Mr. Harris's quote is a fine sentiment I'm sure we all thrive for. Often, everything goes smooth, until one encounters a crack in the pavement and stumbles.

When a student does not do well in a given subject, it can be due to several reasons. Perhaps, the material just did not make sense, because it wasn't explained well. Sometimes there is a personality conflict between the student and the teacher, and at other times the student is preoccupied with current issues and events. Occasionally, students are stressed overall, lacking confidence and are simply overwhelmed by 'lots and lots of puberty'.

And then there are times when students get 'left behind', because either they indulged in prograstination and did not get back in the saddle on time, or they only did 'okay' in a previous class onto which the current subject builds and they are now missing vital skills and information.
