Mr. Harris's quote is a fine sentiment I'm sure we all thrive for. Often, everything goes smooth, until one encounters a crack in the pavement and stumbles.

When a student does not do well in a given subject, it can be due to several reasons. Perhaps, the material just did not make sense, because it wasn't explained well. Sometimes there is a personality conflict between the student and the teacher, and at other times young people are preoccupied, lacking confidence and are simply overwhelmed by 'lots and lots of puberty'.

Occasionally, students get 'left behind', because either they indulged in prograstination and did not get back in the saddle on time, or they only did 'okay' in a previous class onto which the current subject builds and they are now missing vital skills and information.

Algebra Tutoring

Algebra, derived from the Arabic word al-jabr =
the reunion of broken parts, was coined by the
Persian mathematician and astonomer Muhammad
ibn Musa al-Khwarizmi (780-850), who is also
considered 'the father of modern algebra'.
Algebra, the mathmatical manipulation of setting
sides equal to one another, to balance and thereby
find the missing part, to 'reunite and make whole',
has its roots in ancient Egypt and Babylon.
Modern day applications include literally everything.

Geometry Tutoring

Geometry, derived from the ancient Greek words
geo = Earth and metron = measure, was coined by the
ancient Greek Euclid, who is also considered 'the
father of modern geometry'. Geometry, the in-depth
study of shapes, has its roots in ancient Egypt and
Babylon. Modern day applications include literally
everything related to shape.

Trigonometry Tutoring

Trigonometry, derived from the ancient Greek words
trigonon = triangle and metron = measure, was coined
by German astronomer and theologian Bartholomaeus
Piticus (1561 - 1613). Trigonometry, the in-depth study
of the relationship between angles and leg-lengths of
triangles, has its roots in ancient Egypt and Babylon,
though the ancient Greek Hipparchus is considered 'the
father of modern trigonometry'. Modern day applications
include engineering, aviation, navigation, astronomy,
geography, manufacturing, medical imaging, etc.

Calculus Tutoring

Calculus, derived from the ancient Greek word khalix
= pebble, was coined by the German philosopher,
mathmatician, scientist and diplomat Gottfried Leibniz
(1646 - 1716). Calculus, the in-depth study of change
over time, was invented by Gottfried Leibniz and Isaac
Newton simultaneously and independently of one another.
Modern day applications include everything.

Meeting Places


In person meetings occur at a local library or coffee shop. It is best not to meet at a students home,
because there are too many distractions and the balance between student and tutor is out of sync.

Online meetings occur via google meet (audio & video) and jamboard (online whiteboard).
It is not necessary to have a google workplace account. Once a date/time is set, I will
send an adress via email that will allow the student to join the meeting.
