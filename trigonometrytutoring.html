A Bit Of Background



Trigonometry, derived from the ancient Greek words trigonon = triangle and metron = measure, was coined
by German astronomer and theologian Bartholomaeus Piticus (1561 - 1613).

Trigonometry, the in-depth study of the relationship between angles and leg-lengths of
triangles, has its roots in ancient Egypt and Babylon, though the ancient Greek Hipparchus is
considered 'the father of modern trigonometry'.
Modern day applications include engineering, aviation, navigation, astronomy, geography, manufacturing, medical imaging, etc.
