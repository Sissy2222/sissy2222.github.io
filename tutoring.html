Additional Info
       
It is best to nip any rough spots in the bud as soon as they occur. This will prevent the vicious cycle of stress, prograstination
and avoidance. Real tutoring is a bit different from learning in a classroom. Here, the objectives are deeper understanding,
comprehension, assimilation & learning how to become a problem solver. These are key to doing well in any subject.

       
New information is fun, but when the concepts are not explained well, it will lead to non-comprehension

= confusion & stress
  
Often, students are provided with formulas and directions on how to apply these. This can be rewarding, until the parameters change

= apprehension & hesitation
  
Actually understanding the underlying dynamics, the 'Why" it is so and the 'How' it came to be so, changes everything

= zeal, growth & freedom
       

I feel the latter is best accomplished by engaging students (auditorily, visually and verbally - kinestheticly when appropriate), by
working on conundrums together and talking with, rather than talking at or talking down to (lecturing). By asking questions that
provoke thought process, allowing students to connect the dots, mull things over, create their own understanding. By leading and when
s/he has caught up/is ready, allowing them to try and take the lead. By allowing them to play an active role in their own success.
