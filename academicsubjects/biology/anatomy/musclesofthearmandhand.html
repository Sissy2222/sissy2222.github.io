Anconeus

Meaning


ancon = elbow

Function


extends elbow &
rotates forearm

Origin


epicondyle (protuberance above
or on the rounded end of a long
bone) of the humerus

Insertion


bony prominence of ulna

spaceholder

_________________________

Biceps brachii

Meaning


biceps = double-headed
or two peaks
brachii = of the arm

Function


supinates (turning, so palm
faces up) forearm,
flexes elbow & shoulder

Origin


2 loci: above and in the
coracoid process (short
projection from shoulder blade)

Insertion


2 loci: tuberosity (rounded
prominence) of the radius
and fascia of forearm


_________________________

Brachialis

Meaning


brachi = upper arm
from shoulder to elbow

Function


flexes the elbow

Origin


distal (away from center
of body) half of the humerus

Insertion


coracoid process (short
projection from shoulder blade)

_________________________

Brachioradialis

Meaning


brachi = upper arm from shoulder to elbow
radialis = along the radius bone


Function


pronates, supinates
and flexes forearm

Origin


above the condylar
ridge of the humerus
(lower humurus end)

Insertion


bony projection of distal radius
(radial styloid process)

_________________________

Coracobrachialis

Meaning


coraco = ravens beak
brachi = upper arm from shoulder to elbow

Function


flexes & adducts arm,
rotates humerus

Origin


the coracoid process

Insertion


brachial region (humurus)

_________________________

Deltoid

Meaning


deltoeides = triangular

Function


flexes & rotates shoulder,
abducts arm

Origin


clavicle (collarbone),
scapula (shoulder blade),
& spine

Insertion


deltoid tuberosity
(rounded prominence of
upper arm/bone)

_________________________

Infraspinatus

Meaning


infra = below
spinatus = slender projection

Function


rotate humerus,
stabilizes shoulder joint

Origin


intraspinatus fossa of scapula
(shoulder blade)

Insertion


greater protuberance
of the humerus

_________________________

Palmaris longus

Meaning


palma = palm of or
flat of the hand
longus = long

Function


flexes wrist, tenses
palmar aponeurosis (sheet
of pearly white fibrous tissue)

Origin


epicondyle (protuberance above
or on the rounded end of a long
bone) of the humerus

Insertion


palmar aponeurosis (sheet of
pearly white fibrous tissue)

_________________________

Pronator quadratus

Meaning


pronare = to bend forward
quad = square shaped

Function


maintains proper distance
between ulna and radius,
while rotating the forearm

Origin


inner part of ulna

Insertion


inner part of radius

_________________________

Pronator teres

Meaning


pronare = to bend forward
teres = round & smooth or cylindrical


Function


rotates lower arm, so
palm can face backwards

Origin


greater protuberance
of the humerus &
coronoid process of ulna

Insertion


middle of radius

_________________________

Subscapularis

Meaning


sub = under or below
scapularis = scapula

Function


adducts humerus, stabilizes
shoulder joint

Origin


subscapular fossa

Insertion


smaller protuberance of humerus

_________________________

Supinator

Meaning


supination = rotation of forearm

Function


rotates forearm, so palm can face up

Origin


epicondyle (protuberance above
or on the rounded end of a long
bone) of the humerus,
fossa of ulna &
supinator crest

Insertion


radial shaft

_________________________

Supraspinatus

Meaning


supra = above or beyond limits
spinatus = spine, here spine of scapula

Function


abducts arm, stabilizes
humeral head

Origin


supraspinous fossa
at top of scapula

Insertion


greater protuberance
of the humerus

_________________________

Teres major


 >

   Meaning


teres = round & smooth or cylindrical
major = great in size or important

Function


rotates & adducts humerus

Origin


outer border &
smaller angle of the scapula

Insertion


bicipital groove of humerus

_________________________

Teres minor

Meaning


teres = round & smooth or cylindrical
minor = lesser

Function


one of the four rotary cuff muscles
rotates & adducts humerus
stabilizes shoulder joint

Origin


outer border of scapula

Insertion


greater protuberance
of the humerus

_________________________

Triceps brachii

Meaning


tricep = three headed
brachii = of the arm

Function


adducts & extends upper arm
extends forearm

Origin


3 loci: greater protuberance
of the scapula, upper & lower
posterior humerus

Insertion


olecranon (bony prominence of
elbow, which form elbow
joint) process of ulna
