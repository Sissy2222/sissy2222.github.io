Grams Of Flourine To Produce Xenon Tetrafluoride

Xenon can be made to react with fluorine gas to produce xenon tetrafluoride.
If 16.30 g of xenon is used, what mass of fluorine, in grams, is needed to react completely with the xenon?



Let's write out the chemical reaction

F2 + Xe  ⇒  XeF4

let's balance this reaction

2 F2 + Xe  ⇒  XeF4

Xenon and Flouride reaction

molecular weights

Xe = 131.29 g/mole

F = 18.99 g/mole



16.30 g Xe
(1 mole Xe) / (131.29 g Xe)
(2 moles F2) / (1 mole Xe)
(37.98 g F2) / (1 moles F2)
= 9.43 g F2


9.43 g F2 are needed to completely react with 16.30 g of Xe
