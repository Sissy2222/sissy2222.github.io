Finding The Emperical Formula

Elemental analysis of a compound showed that it consisted of 54.53% carbon, 9.15% hydrogen and 36.32% oxygen

How many hydrogens appear in the empirical formula of the compound?

A) 2
B) 4
C) 6
D) 9



When dealing with percentages, then 100% = all of the compound and we therefore set this equal to 100 g

  54.53 g C
+   9.15 g H
+ 36.32 g O
100 g


Step One

convert grams to moles


(54.53 g C) / 1
 
(1 mole C) / (12.01 g C)
  = 4.54038 moles C

  (9.15 g H)  / 1
 
  (1 mole H) / (1.01 g H)
  = 9.05941 moles H

(36.32 g O) / 1
 
(1 mole O) / (16.00 g O)
  = 2.27 moles O

 chemical compound

Step Two

divide the moles of each element
by the smallest number of moles

C =
(4.54038 moles) / (2.27 moles)
  = 2.00016 ~2

H =
(9.05941 moles) / (2.27 moles)
  = 3.9909 ~ 4

O =
(2.27 moles) / (2.27 moles)
  = 1




Emperical Formula
C2H4O1

Answer: B
